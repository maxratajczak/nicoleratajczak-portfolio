Community


Yale Core Design Studio with Emily Abruzzo and Peter de Bretteville






My proposition for a Center for Immigrant Services takes on the challenge of finding a balance between public and private, open and enclosed space. Both are necessary for creating a welcoming environment where people from all walks of life feel safe.


Through studies in plan organization, my project creates space that is porous and interconnected while simultaneously hosting a gradient of public to private functions. Careful attention is paid to the practical needs of building structure and support spaces, while juxtaposing geometries add an element of playfulness to the plan.


Main gathering spaces such as a gymnasium, amphitheater, gallery, and offices occupy the center of the plan, while circulation and supporting spaces are accommodated in the in-between space running the perimeter of the building, formed by a double-layer facade system which opens the building up to the community.
